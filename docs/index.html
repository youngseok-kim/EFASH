We consider an exponential family means problem. Suppose \(x_j\)’s are independently drawn from unknown mean \(\mu_j\)’s and known dispersion parameter \(\tau_j\)’s.

\[ x_j|\mu_j,\tau_j \sim A_j(x_j,\tau_j) \exp\left[-\tau_j D_G(x_j\|\mu_j) \right],\quad j=1,\cdots,n \]

\(A_j\) is a base measure and \(D_G\) is a Bregman divergence generated by \(G\).

We prefer to use a vectorized notation:

\[ x|\mu,\tau \sim A(x,\tau) \exp\left[ -\langle \tau, D_G(x\|\mu) \rangle \right] \]

We introduce a scale mixture of conjugate priors:

\[ \mu_j|0,\phi,t \sim \sum_{k=1}^m \phi_k H(0,t_k) \exp\left[ -t_k D_G(0\|\mu_j) \right] \]

where \(H(0,t_k)\) is a partition function and \(\phi_k\)’s are mixture proportions.
